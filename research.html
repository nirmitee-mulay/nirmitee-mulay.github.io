Research

Consciousness, perception, and emotion

My research investigates conscious and unconscious processing using psychophysics, fMRI, and computational approaches. With an interdisciplinary background in biology, physics, and mathematics from IISER Kolkata, I combine experimental rigor with growing interest in computational modeling and theoretical frameworks.

Current PhD Projects

🧠 Unconscious Working Memory with Visual Ensembles

2023 - Present Psychophysics, fMRI, MVPA

Question: Can information be maintained in working memory without conscious awareness?

Approach: We developed a bias-free paradigm using visual ensembles of faces. Instead of asking about individual faces (which introduces bias), we ask about average emotional expression of face sets. Combined with continuous flash suppression to render stimuli invisible, this tests unconscious working memory without confounds of previous studies.

Finding: When response bias is properly controlled, we find no evidence for unconscious working memory—challenging previous claims and highlighting the importance of methodological rigor.

👁️ Metacognition of Unconscious Face Ensembles

2024 - Present Psychophysics, Signal Detection

Question: Can we make accurate metacognitive judgments about unconscious perceptions?

Building on the ensemble paradigm, we investigate whether participants can judge their confidence about ensemble representations rendered invisible. This speaks to debates about higher-order vs first-order theories of consciousness and will inform computational models of metacognition.

💭 Decoded Neurofeedback for Emotional Regulation

2024 - Present Real-time fMRI, MVPA, Neurofeedback

Vision: Using real-time brain decoding to understand and enhance emotional regulation.

We use multivariate pattern analysis to decode emotional states from fMRI, then provide participants with real-time feedback. This investigates how emotional experiences are represented in distributed brain networks and whether people can learn to regulate these representations through neurofeedback.

Past Research

Emotional Experience in Depersonalization

Master's Thesis | Aug 2021 - May 2022 | Prof. Naotsugu Tsuchiya, Monash University

Investigated emotional experiences in depersonalization—a dissociative condition where individuals feel emotionally "numb" despite showing normal physiological responses to emotional stimuli.

Altered Visual Perception in Derealization

Research Project | May 2021 - Nov 2021 | Prof. Naotsugu Tsuchiya, Monash University

Used psychophysical tasks to assess whether subjective visual distortions in derealization correspond to measurable changes in visual perception.

Publication: Kusztor, A., Mulay, N. N., Yamada, M., Hohwy, J., & Tsuchiya, N. (2023). Lifting the veil: Altered visual perception in derealisation.
