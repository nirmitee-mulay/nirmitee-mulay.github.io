## Extended Bio (for About page - 2-3 paragraphs)
I am a pre-doctoral researcher at the Basque Center on Cognition, Brain and Language (BCBL) in San Sebastian, Spain, working under the supervision of Prof. David Soto. My research investigates the boundaries of conscious and unconscious processing, with a particular focus on working memory, visual perception, and emotional experience. Currently, I'm exploring whether information can be maintained in working memory without conscious awareness, how we form metacognitive judgments about unconscious perceptions, and how decoded neurofeedback can help regulate emotional experiences.

My interdisciplinary training at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Kolkata, where I completed an integrated BS-MS with a major in Biology and minor in Physics, has equipped me with a unique perspective bridging experimental and computational approaches. I have extensive experience in psychophysics and fMRI neuroimaging (preprocessing, MVPA/decoding), and I'm increasingly interested in computational modeling and theoretical frameworks—particularly Bayesian approaches and predictive processing accounts of perception and consciousness. My master's thesis work with Prof. Naotsugu Tsuchiya explored altered subjective experiences in depersonalization and derealization, sparking my ongoing fascination with the relationship between neural processing and phenomenal experience.

Beyond the lab, I'm deeply committed to research transparency and rigor. I co-founded Sigma Sessions at BCBL, an initiative promoting replication, reproduction, and rigorous research practices in cognitive neuroscience. I'm also passionate about science communication and visual storytelling, having worked as a designer and illustrator for Neuromatch and various science communication platforms. As I look toward postdoctoral opportunities, I'm excited to deepen my computational expertise and contribute to labs that value methodological rigor, open science, and interdisciplinary collaboration.
